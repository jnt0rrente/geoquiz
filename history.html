Historical Geology

Definition

Historical Geology, or palaeogeology, is a branch of geology which uses its principles to reconstruct the geological history of planet Earth. It works on a planetary temporal scale, and usually works with geological strata to perform its investigations.

The analysis and delimitation of the different geological eras is usually done according to large-scale geological and biological events. For example, the Permic period is considered to end with a mass-extinction event which led into the Triasic.

Earth's History

We can divide the lifetime of our planet, the Earth, on the following eras:

  • Proterozoic Eon:
    • Paleoproterozoic era: 2500 MYA (Million Years Ago)
    • Mesoproterozoic era: 1600 MYA
    • Neoproterozoic era: 1000 MYA
  • Phanerozoic Eon:
    • Paleozoic era: 541 MYA
    • Mesozoic era: 250 MYA
    • Cenozoic era: 66 MYA
